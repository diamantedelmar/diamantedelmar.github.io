[cite: 90] New Pyli village has a central square with traditional cafes and taverns. [cite: 90] A simple ouzeri near the fortress offers drinks, meze, and views. [cite: 91] Entry to the castle is free, though check for any access restrictions. [cite: 92] The village also hosts the Traditional House of Antimachia, a museum recreating early 20th-century island life, and is known for its honey festival. [cite: 93]

Ancient Wonders - The Asklepion: [cite: 99]

No visit to Kos is complete without exploring the Asklepion, the island's most significant archaeological site and a place of profound historical importance. [cite: 99]

  • Significance: Renowned throughout antiquity, this was one of the most important healing sanctuaries dedicated to Asclepius, the god of medicine and health. [cite: 100] It served not only as a place of worship and healing but also as a respected medical school, intrinsically linked to Hippocrates, the Kos-born "Father of Modern Medicine," who is believed to have taught and practiced here. [cite: 101] The serene setting on a verdant hillside overlooking the sea and the Turkish coast was considered integral to the therapeutic process. [cite: 102]
  • Layout: The sanctuary unfolds across three beautifully landscaped terraces connected by monumental staircases. [cite: 103] The first level housed treatment rooms, fountains for ritual purification, Roman-era baths, and possibly the medical school itself. [cite: 103] The second level was focused on worship, featuring the oldest temple ruins (4th C. BC Temple of Asclepius) and the partially restored Temple of Apollo (Asclepius's father). [cite: 104] A grand staircase leads to the third level, the site of the largest temple dedicated to Asclepius, flanked by porticoes (stoas) likely used for accommodating patients and visitors. [cite: 105] Remains of an early Christian church also exist here. [cite: 106] Above this level lies a forest dedicated to Apollo, offering magnificent views. [cite: 106]
  • Visiting: The Asklepion is conveniently located just 4km from Kos Town. [cite: 107] You can reach it easily by rental car, taxi, the local DEAS bus (City Line 3 or 6 from Kos Town, check schedules), or even on foot for the more energetic (approx. 50 mins). [cite: 107] Allow at least 1-2 hours to explore the archaeological site thoroughly; add extra time if you plan to visit the adjacent Hippocrates Foundation Museum and Botanical Gardens (separate admission). [cite: 108, 109] To fully grasp the site's history and significance, hiring a licensed guide is highly recommended. [cite: 110] Due to the heat and potential crowds, visiting early in the morning or later in the afternoon is advisable during peak summer months (July-August). [cite: 111] Spring and autumn offer particularly atmospheric conditions. [cite: 112] *Important Note:* Reports suggest the site currently lacks disabled access. [cite: 112]
  • Hours & Tickets:
    • Summer (approx. April 1 - Oct 31): Open daily from 08:00 to 20:00. [cite: 113] Last entry is typically 30 minutes before closing (19:30). [cite: 114]
    • Winter (approx. Nov 1 - Mar 31): Open 08:30 to 15:30. [cite: 114] Closed on Tuesdays. [cite: 115]
    • Ticket Price (Summer): €8. [cite: 115]
    • Ticket Price (Winter): €4 (Reduced rate applies to all visitors in winter). [cite: 116]
    • Reductions: EU citizens aged 6-25 enter free; non-EU citizens aged 6-25 get 50% off. [cite: 117] EU citizens over 65 get 50% off. [cite: 118] Valid ID/proof of age/status required. [cite: 118]
    • Combined Ticket: A special package ticket costing €15 is available, valid for 3 days, granting entry to the Asklepion, the Archaeological Museum of Kos, and the Casa Romana. [cite: 119] This offers good value if you plan to visit all three major sites. [cite: 120]
    • A Hellenic Heritage gift shop is located on-site. [cite: 121]
    • Verification Advised: While these details are based on recent information, it's always best practice to quickly verify current hours and prices on the official Hellenic Heritage website (hhticket.gr) or by calling the site before your visit, especially outside peak season. [cite: 122]

Getting Around the Island: [cite: 125]
