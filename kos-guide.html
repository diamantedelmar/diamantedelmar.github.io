Local Products of Kos

Kos is rich in local produce and traditional goods that make for wonderful souvenirs. Here are some of our favorites:

Kos Honey

Kos is known for its excellent thyme honey, rich in aroma and flavor. It's a natural sweetener and a healthy treat.

Where to buy: Local markets, small shops in villages like Zia, and specialty food stores. Look for jars labeled "Μέλι Θυμαρίσιο" (Thyme Honey).

Posa Cheese (Wine Cheese)

A unique local cheese, "Posa" (or "Tyri tis Posas") is traditionally aged in red wine sediment, giving it a distinctive reddish hue and a rich, spicy flavor.

Where to buy: Local dairies, larger supermarkets, and some traditional tavernas. Ask for "Τυρί της Πόσας".

Tomato Spoon Sweet (Glyko Tomataki)

A traditional Greek "spoon sweet" made from small, unripe tomatoes. It's candied and has a unique, delightful flavor, often served with coffee or as a dessert.

Where to buy: Local women's cooperatives, specialty food stores, and some larger supermarkets. Look for jars labeled "Γλυκό Ντοματάκι".

5-Day Kos Itinerary

Here's a suggested 5-day itinerary to help you make the most of your visit to Kos Island, blending history, relaxation, and local experiences.

Day 1: Ancient History & Kos Town Charm

  • Morning: Explore the Castle of Neratzia at the harbor entrance.
  • Lunch: Enjoy fresh seafood at a taverna by the old port.
  • Afternoon: Wander through the ancient Agora and visit the Roman Odeon.
  • Evening: Stroll through the charming streets of Kos Town, enjoy dinner at a local restaurant, and perhaps a drink at a bar in Eleftherias Square.

Day 2: Healing History & Mountain Village Sunset

  • Morning: Visit the Asklepion, the ancient healing center associated with Hippocrates.
  • Lunch: Have lunch in the picturesque mountain village of Zia.
  • Afternoon: Explore Zia's artisan shops and enjoy the panoramic views.
  • Evening: Experience a breathtaking sunset from Zia, followed by dinner at one of its traditional tavernas.

Day 3: Southern Beaches & Thermal Springs

  • Morning: Head to the south coast for a day of relaxation at Paradise Beach, known for its golden sand and clear waters.
  • Lunch: Grab a bite at a beachside taverna at Paradise Beach.
  • Afternoon: Visit Therma Beach and enjoy a natural spa experience in the hot springs.
  • Evening: Return to Kos Town for a relaxed dinner or try a new spot near your accommodation.

Day 4: Western Charm & Traditional Villages

  • Morning: Drive to the western side of the island to explore the traditional village of Kefalos and its old town.
  • Lunch: Enjoy lunch with a view at a taverna overlooking Kefalos Bay.
  • Afternoon: Relax at Agios Stefanos Beach, with its iconic islet of Kastri and its small church.
  • Evening: Head back towards Kos Town, perhaps stopping in a village like Antimachia for a traditional Greek dinner.

Day 5: Relaxation & Last-Minute Souvenirs

  • Morning: Enjoy a leisurely morning at Lambi Beach, close to Kos Town, for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Lunch: Casual lunch at a beach bar or cafe in Lambi.
  • Afternoon: Last-minute souvenir shopping in Kos Town or revisit a favorite spot.
  • Evening: Farewell dinner at a special restaurant in Kos Town, reflecting on your amazing trip.

Island Hopping from Kos

Kos is an excellent base for exploring nearby islands. Here are some popular options for day trips:

Nisyros Island: The Volcanic Gem

Nisyros is a small, picturesque island dominated by its active volcano, one of the few accessible hydrothermal craters in the world.

  • What to do: Visit the Stefanos Crater (walk inside!), explore the charming capital of Mandraki with its white-washed houses and the Monastery of Panagia Spiliani.
  • Ferry Schedule: Daily departures from Kos Town port. Journey time is approximately 1-1.5 hours.
  • Booking: Tickets can be purchased at the port from various tour operators or online.

3-Island Day Excursion: Pserimos, Kalymnos & Plati

A very popular full-day boat trip from Kos Harbor, offering a taste of three different Dodecanese islands.

  • Pserimos: Enjoy the tranquil main beach and small village.
  • Kalymnos: Explore Pothia, the capital, known for its sponge diving history and vibrant harbor.
  • Plati: A tiny uninhabited islet with stunning turquoise waters, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Booking: Numerous boats offer this excursion directly from Kos Harbor. You can usually book on the spot.
