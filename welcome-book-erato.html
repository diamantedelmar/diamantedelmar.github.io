Village Charm - Beyond the Capital

  • Zia: (Must-visit, especially for sunset)
    • Location: 16km S of Kos Town.
    • Highlights: Breathtaking panoramic sunset views. Whitewashed buildings, craft shops.
    • Dining: Oromedon (stunning views, local cuisine - mountain pork, moussaka).
    • Activities: Kos Natural Park, hike Mount Dikeos.
    • Tip: Book restaurants/transport for sunset in advance; sunset bus tours available.
  • Kefalos:
    • Location: 40km SW of Kos Town.
    • Highlights: Quieter, traditional base. Picturesque village on a hill overlooking Kamari Bay and Kastri islet. Narrow alleys, traditional houses. Ancient capital (Astypalea).
    • Sites: Kefalos Castle ruins, Basilica of Saint Stephen.
    • Dining: Mylotopi (windmill cafe), Taverna Niotis (no-menu Greek experience), Taverna Cavos (fresh fish), Ficus (modern).
    • Activities: Hub for southern beaches, Aspri Petra cave.
  • Pyli:
    • Location: 15km SW of Kos Town.
    • Highlights: Nearby Paleo Pyli (Byzantine fortress ruins with views). Central square with cafes/taverns.
    • Dining: Simple ouzeri near fortress. Fox cafe (Pyli village, nice cakes e.g., Banoffee).
  • Antimachia:
    • Location: 23km SW of Kos Town, near airport.
    • Highlights: Imposing Venetian Castle of Antimachia (impressive walls, bastions, two small churches - Agios Nikolaos, Agia Paraskevi with frescoes). Traditional House of Antimachia (museum). Known for honey festival.
    • Castle Entry: Free (check access).
  • Asfendiou & Mountain Hamlets (Lagoudi, Asomatos):
    • Location: Slopes of Mt. Dikeos, 14-15km S of Kos Town.
    • Highlights: Authentic, rural Kos. Asfendiou (traditional architecture). Lagoudi (abandoned & refurbished village). Asomatos (untouched by tourism).
    • Dining: Traditional kafeneion in Asomatos (meze, drinks, music). Grandma's House taverna in Lagoudi.
  • Platani:
    • Location: 3km from Kos Town.
    • Highlights: Home of Kos's Turkish-speaking Muslim minority.
    • Dining: Turkish-influenced Sherif restaurant.

Ancient Wonders - The Asklepion

The island's most significant archaeological site.

  • Significance: Important ancient healing sanctuary for Asclepius, god of medicine. Medical school linked to Hippocrates. Serene hillside setting overlooking sea was part of therapy.
  • Layout: Three landscaped terraces.
    • 1st Level: Treatment rooms, fountains, Roman baths, possibly medical school.
    • 2nd Level: Oldest temple ruins (4th C. BC Temple of Asclepius), Temple of Apollo.
    • 3rd Level: Largest temple to Asclepius, porticoes for patients. Remains of early Christian church. Forest of Apollo above.
  • Visiting:
    • Location: 4km from Kos Town.
    • Getting There: Rental car, taxi, DEAS bus (City Line 3 or 6), or walk (50 mins).
    • Time Needed: 1-2 hours for site; extra for Hippocrates Foundation Museum/Gardens (separate admission).
    • Tip: Hire a licensed guide. Visit early morning or late afternoon in summer. Spring/autumn atmospheric.
    • Accessibility: Reports suggest site currently lacks disabled access.
  • Hours & Tickets:
    • Summer (Apr 1 - Oct 31): Daily 08:00-20:00 (last entry 19:30).
    • Winter (Nov 1 - Mar 31): 08:30-15:30 (Closed Tuesdays).
    • Ticket (Summer): €8.
    • Ticket (Winter): €4.
    • Reductions: EU citizens 6-25 free; non-EU 6-25 50% off. EU citizens over 65 50% off. ID required.
    • Combined Ticket: €15 (Asklepion, Arch. Museum, Casa Romana - valid 3 days). Available at hhticket.gr or on site.
    • Gift Shop: Hellenic Heritage gift shop on-site.
    • Verification Advised: Always verify hours/prices on hhticket.gr or by calling.

Section 6: Navigating Kos

Getting Around the Island

  • Rental Car / Scooter / ATV: Recommended for flexibility. Agencies in Kos Town, airport, resorts. Car approx. €35-€60/day; scooter €25/day (varies). Valid license needed (Int. Driving Permit for some non-EU). Book ahead in summer.
  • KTEL Intercity Buses: Economical. Main station: 7 Kleopatras St, Kos Town. Modern, A/C. Tickets on bus (€2.30-€5.20). Timetables vary; check www.ktel-kos.gr or station. Contact: +30 22420 22292.
    • To Kefalos: Daily, 07:30-21:00. €5.20. ~1h 15m.
    • To Zia: Daily, 09:30-15:45. €2.30. ~30m. Limited return buses post-sunset from Zia.
    • To Tigaki: Frequent, 08:10-23:30. €2.30.
    • To Marmari: Frequent, 08:00-23:30. €2.30.
    • To Airport (KGS): Regular connections. ~30-40m. Check KTEL site.
  • Urban Buses (DEAS): For Kos Town & nearby (Lambi, Psalidi, Asklepion, Therma). Stops around town. Check mobility.kos.gr or +30 22420 26276. City Line 5 hourly to Therma (09:15-16:35).
  • Taxis: Available island-wide. Ranks, hail, or book (Kos Town: +30 22420 23333 / 22777; KosTaxi.eu, KosRadioTaxi.gr). Metered; confirm fare for long journeys. Kos Town-Kefalos ~€50-€60; Kos Town-Zia ~€19-€23.
  • Cycling: Pleasant for shorter distances (flat North/East). Cycle paths Kos Town to Tigaki & Lambi/Psalidi. Rentals €5-€10/day.
