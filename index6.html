Natural Beauty of Kos

For its relatively small size, Kos is an island of incredible natural diversity. From salt lakes and wetlands to volcanic springs and lush forests, there's so much to explore beyond the beaches.

Aliki Salt Lake, Tigaki

A stunning coastal wetland that was once used for salt production. Today, it's a protected nature reserve, bustling with life, especially during autumn and winter when it becomes a haven for migratory birds like flamingos.

View on Map

Mount Dikaios

Rising to 846 meters, Mount Dikaios offers breathtaking panoramic views of Kos and the surrounding islands. The slopes are covered in pine forests and plane trees, making it a wonderful destination for hiking, especially in the spring when wildflowers are in bloom.

View on Map

Plaka Forest

A beautiful pine forest near Antimachia, famous for its resident peacocks and cats. It's a cool, shaded oasis perfect for a picnic, a gentle walk, and enjoying a bit of tranquility away from the summer heat.

Local Recipes

Take a step out of your comfort zone and try something new, making the most of your holiday on Kos Island with these traditional dishes!

Pligouri (Bulgur) with Pork

A hearty and traditional dish where bulgur wheat is slow-cooked with tender pork, creating a rich and flavorful meal that's a staple in local homes.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork, cut into small cubes
  • 1 cup coarse bulgur wheat (pligouri)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g chopped tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2.5 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the pork and cook until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Rinse the bulgur wheat under cold water and add it to the pot. Stir for a minute to toast it slightly.
  4. Pour in the chopped tomatoes and broth. Season with salt and pepper, and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the bulgur is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid.
  6. Let it stand for 5-10 minutes off the heat before fluffing with a fork and serving.

Ntolmadakia (Stuffed Vine Leaves)

Tender vine leaves are wrapped around a filling of rice and fresh herbs. They can be served warm or cold, often with a slice of lemon and a dollop of yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar (about 250g) of vine leaves in brine
  • 1 cup short-grain rice, rinsed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/2 cup olive oil, divided
  • 2 cups hot water
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Carefully separate the vine leaves and rinse them well. Blanch them in boiling water for 3-5 minutes to soften, then drain.
  2. In a bowl, mix the rice, onion, dill, mint, half of the olive oil, juice of 1 lemon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Lay a vine leaf flat (shiny side down). Place a teaspoon of the filling near the stem. Fold the bottom ends over the filling, then fold in the sides, and roll up tightly. Repeat for all leaves.
  4. Line the bottom of a wide pot with a few extra vine leaves. Arrange the stuffed leaves snugly in layers.
  5. Pour the remaining olive oil, lemon juice, and hot water over the top. Place an inverted heatproof plate on top to keep them from unravelling.
  6. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 45-60 minutes until the rice is cooked. Let them cool in the pot before serving.

Krasotyri Saganaki (Fried Wine Cheese)

A unique local delicacy, pan-fried to perfection. The wine-infused cheese becomes warm and slightly melted on the inside with a delicious crispy crust.

Ingredients:

  • 1 slice of Krasotyri (wine cheese), about 1.5cm thick
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon wedge, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Dredge the slice of krasotyri in the flour, making sure it's fully coated on all sides. Pat off any excess.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a small, non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Carefully place the floured cheese in the hot pan. Fry for 2-3 minutes per side, until a deep golden-brown crust forms.
  4. Remove from the pan and place on a plate. Serve immediately with a generous squeeze of fresh lemon juice over the top.

Kos is rich in local produce and traditional goods that make for wonderful souvenirs. Here are some of our favorites:

Kos Honey

Kos is known for its excellent thyme honey, rich in aroma and flavor. It's a natural sweetener and a healthy treat.

Where to buy: Local markets, small shops in villages like Zia, and specialty food stores. Look for jars labeled "Μέλι Θυμαρίσιο" (Thyme Honey).

Taking it home: Honey is generally safe to carry in your checked luggage. Ensure it's well-sealed to prevent leaks.

Posa Cheese (Wine Cheese)

A unique local cheese, "Posa" (or "Tyri tis Posas") is traditionally aged in red wine sediment, giving it a distinctive reddish hue and a rich, spicy flavor.

Where to buy: Local dairies, larger supermarkets, and some traditional tavernas. Ask for "Τυρί της Πόσας".

Taking it home: Best transported in checked luggage, ideally in a vacuum-sealed pack if available from the vendor, to maintain freshness and prevent odors.

Tomato Spoon Sweet (Glyko Tomataki)

A traditional Greek "spoon sweet" made from small, unripe tomatoes. It's candied and has a unique, delightful flavor, often served with coffee or as a dessert.

Where to buy: Local women's cooperatives, specialty food stores, and some larger supermarkets. Look for jars labeled "Γλυκό Ντοματάκι".

Taking it home: Jars are best in checked luggage. Ensure the lid is tight to prevent leakage.

Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Greece is famous for its olive oil, and Kos produces excellent quality extra virgin olive oil. It's a staple in Greek cuisine and a healthy choice.

Where to buy: Directly from olive oil producers (some offer tours), local markets, and supermarkets. Look for "Εξαιρετικό Παρθένο Ελαιόλαδο".

Taking it home: For larger quantities, checked luggage is necessary. Many shops sell olive oil in specially designed, leak-proof tins or bottles for travel.

Local Herbs and Spices

The island's climate is perfect for growing aromatic herbs like oregano, thyme, and sage. These make for fragrant and practical souvenirs.

Where to buy: Local markets, souvenir shops, and even some supermarkets. Look for dried herbs in small bags or decorative bundles.

Taking it home: Can be carried in both carry-on and checked luggage. Ensure they are well-packaged to retain their aroma and avoid spillage.

Handmade Ceramics

You'll find beautiful handmade pottery and ceramics throughout Kos, often featuring traditional Greek designs or vibrant colors. From decorative plates to functional mugs, they make lovely keepsakes.

Where to buy: Artisan shops in Kos Town and villages, particularly in areas with a craft tradition.

Taking it home: Pack fragile items carefully in your checked luggage, using clothing or bubble wrap for cushioning. Consider shipping larger or very delicate pieces.

Island Hopping from Kos

Kos is an excellent base for exploring nearby islands. Here are some popular options for day trips:

Nisyros Island: The Volcanic Gem

Nisyros is a small, picturesque island dominated by its active volcano, one of the few accessible hydrothermal craters in the world.

  • What to do: Visit the Stefanos Crater (walk inside!), explore the charming capital of Mandraki with its white-washed houses and the Monastery of Panagia Spiliani.
  • Ferry Schedule: Daily departures from Kos Town port, usually in the morning (e.g., 9:00 AM - 10:00 AM) with return in the late afternoon (e.g., 3:00 PM - 4:00 PM). Journey time is approximately 1-1.5 hours.
  • Booking: Tickets can be purchased at the port from various tour operators or online. It's advisable to book in advance during peak season.

Pserimos Island: Tranquil Escape

Pserimos is a small, quiet island known for its beautiful sandy beach and crystal-clear waters, perfect for a relaxing day away from the crowds.

  • What to do: Swim and sunbathe on the main beach, enjoy fresh fish at one of the few tavernas, and experience the peaceful island life.
  • Ferry Schedule: Often part of a "3-Island Cruise" from Kos.
  • Booking: Tickets usually purchased directly from vendors at Kos Town port.

3-Island Day Excursion: Pserimos, Kalymnos & Plati

A very popular full-day boat trip from Kos Harbor, offering a taste of three different Dodecanese islands.

  • Pserimos: Enjoy the tranquil main beach and small village.
  • Kalymnos: Explore Pothia, the capital, known for its sponge diving history and vibrant harbor.
  • Plati: A tiny uninhabited islet with stunning turquoise waters, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Schedule: Boats typically depart Kos Harbor between 9:30 AM and 10:30 AM, returning around 4:30 PM to 5:30 PM. Includes stops at each island for swimming and exploration.
  • Booking: Numerous boats offer this excursion directly from Kos Harbor. You can usually book on the spot the day before or the morning of departure. Prices vary by boat and inclusions (e.g., lunch).
